XYNTHESIZR

Xynthesizr is like ToneMatrix on steroids.
It's an iPhone app that uses a grid to sequence a full-fledged polyphonic synthesizer.

  • Synthesizer Features

    • 16 voices (12 on iPhone 3GS)
    • 2 oscillators per voice
    • 4 selectable waveforms (sine, triangle, sawtooth, square) for each oscillator
    • detune control for one of the oscillators
    • 4-pole (24 dB/oct) low-pass resonant filter
    • separate ADSR envelope generators for amplitude and filter frequency
    • 2 LFOs
    • 4 selectable LFO targets (filter frequency, pitch, amplitude, oscillator detune)
    • audio FX including reverb and randomized panner

  • Sequencer Features

    • grid with maximum size of 32x26 (4-inch screen devices) or 32x22 (3.5-inch screen devices)
    • selectable number of steps (1-32)
    • BPM range from 1 to 300
    • selectable keys and scales (X built-in scales)
    • ability to create your own scales (available as In-App Purchase)
    • ability to 'tie' consecutive notes of same pitch (effectively making them one long note)
    • 2 pattern morphing algorithms
      a) randomized
      b) based on Life-like cellular automaton (including, but not limited to Conway's Game of Life)
    • ability to create your own sets of rules for the cellular automaton (available as In-App Purchase)
    • ability to make the morphing algorithm work in either the active area of a pattern or all 32 steps
